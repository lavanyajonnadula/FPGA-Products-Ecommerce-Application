FPGA (Field Programmable Gate Array) boards are reconfigurable digital logic devices used for custom hardware development.
They contain logic blocks and interconnects that can be programmed to perform complex functions.
Developers use HDLs like Verilog or VHDL to define the hardware behavior.
FPGAs are widely used in embedded systems, signal processing, and communication protocols.
They enable parallel processing, making them faster than microcontrollers for certain tasks.
FPGAs are ideal for prototyping digital circuits, allowing hardware changes without manufacturing.
Common applications include image processing, robotics, cryptography, and custom processors.
In real-time, FPGAs are used in aerospace, automotive (ADAS systems), and medical devices.
They are also critical in 5G base stations, IoT edge devices, and high-frequency trading systems.
Popular boards like Zybo Z7, DE10-Nano, and Basys 3 support both academic learning and industrial projects.
